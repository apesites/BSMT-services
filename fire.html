Fire Protection

Fire Extinguisher & Portable Fire Solutions

Fire equipment referes to various devices and systems used to detect, control and extinguish fires. Servicing of fire equiment involves regular maintanance and inspections to ensure that these devices and systems are in good working conditions and perform as intended in case of a fire.

Roof & In Rack Fire Springler Systems

Commercial fire springler systems are automatic fire suppression systems designed to control fire in a building or structure. Commercial fire springler systems are typically required by local building codes and fire codes for larger or high occupancy buildings, and are an important part of overall fire safety in commercial structures.

Hydrostatic Pressure Testing

Hydrostatic pressure testing is a method used to ensure the safety and Reliability for CO2 fire extinguishers by checking the strength and integrity of the cylinder. The test involves filling the cylinder with water and applying increasing pressure to it untill the desired test pressure is reached.

Commercial Kitchen Sytems

Commercial Kitchen suppression systems are fire suppression systems designed specifically for commercial Kitchens to control and extinguish cooking fires. They typically use a combination of chemicals and fire extinguishing agents, such as water, foam, or dry chemicals, to suppress fires.

Maintanance & systems overhaul

Small works Springler maintanance typically includes tasks such as checking and cleaning springler heads, checking, repairing and leaks, adjusting the spray pattern to ensure proper coverage, and testing the system to ensure its functioning properly and ensuring the springler systems is in good working condition.

Fire Detection & Suppression Systems

Fire detection and supression refers to the methods and systems used to detect and extinguish fires. Fire detection systems typically include smoke detectors, and fire alarms that alert people when a fire is detected. Fire suppression sytems on the other hand, refers to the active measures taken to extinguish or control fire.

How often should fire extinguishers be serviced?

Fire equipment should be serviced every 12 months (yearly) by a registered company. Products are serviced by a BBI or SABS approved Company and SAQCC registered technician, the technicians registration number should be indicated on the service label. (You can check the service technician or companies on the following website: www.saqccfire.co.za).

What should you consider when selecting fire equipment?

All equipment being installed or serviced should carry the BSI or SABS markings as it relates to SANS accreditation.

What are the pressure test intervals for the extinguishers?

All the extinguishers require pressure testing by law. Foam, water and powder extinguishers require pressure testing every 5 years. CO2 extinguishers need to be pressure tested within the first 5 years and then require pressure testing every 10 years.

How often does my sprinkler system need to be serviced?

Your springler systems need to be inspected on a monthly basis by a competent person within your organization. The pumps for any sprinkler system need to be serviced every 12 months (yearly) basis by a registered and approved competent person. The valves need to be overhauled every 3 years by a registered and approved competent person.

How often does my detection and suppression system need to be serviced?

Your fire systems need to be inspected on a monthly basis by a competent person within your organization. it is recommended that fire systems are serviced every 3 months (quarterly) by a registered and approved competent person.
